artist bio

Rafaël Rozendaal (1980) is a Dutch-Brazilian artist who currently lives in New York. He uses the internet as his canvas. Rozendaal’s artistic practice comprises animations, websites, NFTs, installations, tapestries, prints, writing and since 2023, painting. His work takes shape through a range of transformations – from movement into abstraction, from virtual into physical space, and from website and NFTs to print – with all of them informing each other.Rozendaal’s installations involve moving light and reflections, taking online works and transforming them into spatial experiences.His work has been exhibited at the Whitney Museum of American Art, Nam June Paik Art Center Seoul, Centre Pompidou, Stedelijk Museum, and Venice Biennale among others.

key characteristics of the artist's work

The part that makes his artwork unique is that he uses simple shapes and creates an illusion, and all of his works have one thing in common, which is that they stem from a fascination with moving images and interactivity in its most basic form. He will use many different colors to make his artwork more visually appealing. Also, is look like the shapes he use the more square and circular. When he keeps using the square shape in his artwork, it makes it look like pixel artwork. All of his works have a sense of movement and interactivity that engages the viewer. And he makes the artwork that is coming to life because of the movement in the artwork. And the colors he picked for the artwork match each other very well. You can see it aesthetically when you look at the image longer because you will feel like you're moving with the image

critical analyst

The reason I chose to write about this artist is that the artwork is unique and engaging. Also, the artwork looks like it's going to trick your brain, like it's moving when you look at it. Also, the color the artist picked for its artwork looks very appealing. The three artworks I picked are the ones I like because of the color, and they'll illustrate what you can see from the artworks. The window artwork makes you feel like you are part of the artwork by having interactivity in the artwork, which, when you move the glass in front of you, you will hear bird sounds from the background, and when you close it back, the bird sounds disappear. The Quadrant artwork, it makes you feel like you're in a rainbow box with no end when you look deeper inside. The dive artwork makes you feel you have mesmerized when you keep looking at it.
